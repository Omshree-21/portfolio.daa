Flood Management Algorithms

1. Why Use Dijkstra's Algorithm?

Dijkstra's Algorithm is ideal for finding the shortest path in weighted graphs, such as road networks in flood-affected areas. It ensures:

  • Efficient routing for rescue operations.
  • Avoidance of heavily flooded or unsafe routes.
  • Optimization of travel time and safety for evacuations.

2. Why Use Breadth-First Search (BFS)?

BFS is excellent for exploring all reachable areas in unweighted graphs. It is particularly useful because:

  • It identifies the shortest path in unweighted scenarios.
  • It helps prioritize evacuation from accessible regions.
  • It provides a clear view of connected and affected zones.
Transportation Problem Solutions

1. Floyd-Warshall Algorithm

This algorithm calculates the shortest paths between all pairs of nodes in a weighted graph.

  • Ideal for optimizing routes in transportation networks.
  • Minimizes overall transportation costs by evaluating all possible paths.

2. Kruskal's Algorithm

This algorithm constructs a minimum spanning tree by adding edges with the lowest weights.

  • Efficiently connects nodes with minimal transportation costs.
  • Helps in building an optimized transportation network.
